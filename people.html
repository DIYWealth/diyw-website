John Walker

Co-founder

John studied Mathematics at the University of Warwick, followed by a master’s degree in Mathematical Modelling and Scientific Computing at the University of Oxford. He then joined Orbis Investment Management as a Performance Analyst in 2009, where he completed Level 1 of the Charted Financial Analyst program. His experiences at Orbis fermented his belief that a strategy for the average investor should be simple, intuitive, and unemotional, which DIYWealth aims to achieve.

In 2011 John returned to academia to pursue his interest in particle physics by studying for a master’s in Physics at Imperial College London. John subsequently joined the University of Liverpool to study for a PhD in High Energy Physics as part of the SNO+ neutrinoless double-beta decay experiment, located in an underground laboratory in a mine in Northern Ontario.

John is currently based in Vancouver, working as a postdoctoral research fellow at TRIUMF, Canada's particle accelerator centre, in conjunction with the University of Winnipeg. His field of research is neutrino oscillation physics, and is a member of the Japan-based T2K and Super-Kamiokande experiments as well as the next generation Hyper-Kamiokande proto-collaboration. These experiments involve sending underground beams of neutrinos across Japan and detecting their interactions in large tanks of water. Current interests include machine learning applications for event reconstruction.

John is a keen musician, playing saxophone and piano, whose enthusiasm compensates for his competence. He is also attempting to learn Spanish in his free time.
